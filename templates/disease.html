What is COPD?

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a chronic inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs. It is a progressive condition, meaning it typically worsens over time.

Main Causes

The primary cause of COPD is long-term exposure to irritating gases or particulate matter, most often from cigarette smoke. Other contributors include air pollution, chemical fumes, and dust in work environments.

How It Affects the Body

COPD includes conditions like emphysema and chronic bronchitis. It causes symptoms such as difficulty breathing, cough, mucus production, and wheezing. In people with COPD, the airways and air sacs lose their elastic quality, and the walls between air sacs can be destroyed.

Who is at Risk?

People at risk include smokers, those with a family history of lung disease, and individuals who have prolonged exposure to environmental pollutants. Genetics can also play a role through deficiencies like alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency.

Living with COPD

While COPD cannot be cured, it can be managed. Treatments include medications (such as bronchodilators), pulmonary rehabilitation, and in some cases, oxygen therapy or surgery. Lifestyle changes like quitting smoking, exercising, and a healthy diet are crucial.

Importance of Early Detection

Detecting COPD early allows for timely interventions that can slow the progression of the disease and significantly improve quality of life. Our tool helps assess the likelihood of COPD to support early action.
