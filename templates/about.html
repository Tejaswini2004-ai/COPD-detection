üü° Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • What is COPD? COPD is a progressive lung disease that causes breathing difficulties. It is usually caused by smoking or long-term exposure to harmful pollutants.
  • How do I know if I have COPD? Common symptoms of COPD include persistent cough, shortness of breath, wheezing, and fatigue. If you have these symptoms and are a smoker or have been exposed to lung irritants, we recommend seeing a doctor for a diagnosis.
  • Can COPD be cured? Currently, there is no cure for COPD. However, with the right treatment and lifestyle changes, you can manage symptoms and improve quality of life.
  • How can I prevent COPD? The best way to prevent COPD is to avoid smoking or quit if you currently smoke. Additionally, minimizing exposure to pollutants and staying active can help protect your lung health.
  • What treatments are available for COPD? Treatment options for COPD include medications (e.g., bronchodilators, steroids), pulmonary rehabilitation, oxygen therapy, and in some cases, surgery. The treatment plan is customized based on the severity of the disease.
