What are Genetic Markers?
Genetic markers are specific sequences in your DNA that can vary from person to person. These variations, known as Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs), may influence your health. Our model uses 9 key SNPs related to inflammation, immune response, and lung function, which can affect your chances of developing Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD).

üîç How Do You Know Your Genetic Marker Values?

  • If you've done a DNA test from 23andMe, AncestryDNA, MyHeritage, or any clinical test, you can download your raw genetic data as a text or CSV file.
  • Use tools like Promethease or Codegen to analyze the raw data.
  • Search for the SNP (e.g., rs20541) in your raw data. You'll see values like AA, AG, or GG.

üßæ How to Select the Right Option

For each SNP input in the form, choose based on your genotype:

  • 0 = AA (homozygous for A allele)
  • 1 = AG (heterozygous ‚Äî one A, one G)
  • 2 = GG (homozygous for G allele)

If you find a different combination like CT or TC, treat it the same way: pick 0 (both same), 1 (mixed), or 2 (both other base).
